Chapter 1: Recognizing Phishing

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  • Identify suspicious sender addresses
  • Recognize urgent or threatening language
  • Spot generic greetings and poor personalization
  • Detect suspicious links and attachments

Red Flags in Phishing Emails

1. Suspicious Sender Addresses
⚠️ Example:
security@paypa1.com (note the "1" instead of "l")
noreply@bank-security.net (suspicious domain)
2. Urgent Language
⚠️ Example:
"URGENT: Your account will be suspended in 24 hours!"
"Immediate action required to avoid account closure"
3. Generic Greetings
⚠️ Example:
"Dear Customer" instead of your actual name
"Dear Valued User" or "Dear Account Holder"
4. Suspicious Links
⚠️ Example:
Links that don't match the claimed sender
Shortened URLs (bit.ly, tinyurl) in official emails

Interactive Exercise

Can you spot the phishing attempt?
From: security@arnazon-support.com
Subject: URGENT: Verify Your Account Now!

Dear Customer,

We have detected suspicious activity on your account. Please click the link below to verify your identity immediately or your account will be suspended within 24 hours.

Verify Account Now

Thank you,
Amazon Security Team
Red Flags Identified:
  • Misspelled domain: "arnazon" instead of "amazon"
  • Generic greeting: "Dear Customer"
  • Urgent threatening language
  • Suspicious call-to-action button
